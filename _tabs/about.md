# About RecEngine

RecEngine Tech Blog is where our team writes down the engineering lessons that
usually stay trapped in pull requests, whiteboards, and launch retrospectives.

We work on the systems that help products surface the right content, action, or
decision at the right moment. That means we spend a lot of time thinking about
ranking quality, retrieval latency, experimentation, feature pipelines, model
operations, and the developer workflows needed to keep all of it moving.

## What We Write About

- Recommendation and ranking architecture
- Search, retrieval, and feature engineering
- ML platform and evaluation tooling
- Backend systems, observability, and performance
- Product engineering stories from ideas to production

## Why This Blog Exists

We wanted a lightweight place to publish:

- Technical deep dives after major projects
- Practical notes from incidents and debugging sessions
- Design tradeoffs that shaped our architecture
- Reusable patterns the wider team can build on

> We aim for posts that are concrete, honest, and useful to engineers who are
> building real systems under real constraints.
{: .prompt-info }

## Editorial Principles

- Show the tradeoffs, not just the polished outcome
- Prefer production lessons over abstract theory
- Keep examples grounded in code, data, and user impact
- Leave enough detail that a teammate could reuse the idea later

## Follow Along

The blog is built with Jekyll and the Chirpy theme, and it is intended to grow
with the team over time as we publish architecture notes, launch writeups, and
engineering retrospectives.
